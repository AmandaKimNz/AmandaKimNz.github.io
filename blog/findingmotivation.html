Finding Motivation to Write

Main About Me Works Blog

Sometimes, I have a really great idea for a story, and I just sit down and throw it all onto a page. All's well and good. But most of the time, I either have no idea what to write, or I just keep finding myself distracted until the day is over and I have to sleep. And the older I get, the more I feel like I've been getting distracted. I used to be able to pump out three pages in an afternoon, but now I'm lucky to get one.

I think some part of it is that I've been dealing with my own personal issues (which I won't go into here), and that's been killing my mood in general. And semi-related, but I'm in university as I'm writing this. In fact, that's why I'm writing this. It's work, and after a day of genuine work I really want to relax with video games or shows, not trying to force myself to write.

It's not that I don't enjoy writing. Once I've started, I find it fun. It's just that "getting started" bit that's hard for me. It sounds like too much hard work given my limited free time and how much I've already done that day.

One thing that's helped me is actually making writing work. Like, if I have a deadline (if it's for a friend, commission, or contest), I can write it no problem. It doesn't even feel too much like work, it just gives me that little bit of pressure I need.

Another thing I've found recently is this thing called "Writing Sprints". It's a Discord bot (but I'm sure you could do it on your own as well) that will time you for 15-20 minutes, and you have to write as much as you can. In the end, you submit your word count. It doesn't really reward you with anything (outside of a "good job"), but I've found having that deadline helps me focus a lot. You won't believe how much more productive I became after finding out about those.

But writing sprints are mostly effective for me because my other writing friends are able to join in. It becomes something of a social experience, despite none of us talking to each other during the sprint. I can't even explain why, it's just easier to write knowing someone else is writing alongside me on something completely unrelated.

So, yeah. I have motivation issues, sprints are my best way to fight against that.
